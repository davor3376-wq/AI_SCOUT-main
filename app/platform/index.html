Welcome to AI SCOUT

AI SCOUT is an evidence-grade satellite intelligence platform. It transforms raw Copernicus satellite data into auditable, legally-defensible environmental intelligence — complete with SHA-256 provenance hashing and PDF reports.
  • 1

  • 2

Step 2 of 5
Draw Your Area of Interest
⬡
Use the draw toolbar on the right side of the map to define the geographic area you want to monitor.
  • ▭
    Rectangle tool — click and drag to define a bounding box
  • ⬡
    Polygon tool — click multiple points to draw any shape, double-click to finish
  • ◎
    The AOI indicator in the sidebar confirms your selection with coordinates

You can edit or delete your AOI at any time using the edit controls in the draw toolbar.

Step 3 of 5
Configure Mission Parameters
≡
Set your Date Range and choose a Sensor type in the sidebar:
  • O
    OPTICAL — Sentinel-2 visible-spectrum imagery. Best for clear-sky conditions.
  • R
    RADAR — Sentinel-1 SAR. Penetrates cloud cover, works day and night.
  • N
    NDVI vegetation health  ·  NDWI water extent  ·  NBR burn severity
  • S
    S1 RTC — Terrain-corrected radar for precise land-change analysis.

Optionally set Schedule to DAILY for recurring automated missions.

Step 4 of 5
Launch a Mission
▶
Click LAUNCH MISSION at the bottom of the sidebar to submit your analysis.
  • 1
    Status changes to RUNNING — the system is fetching and processing satellite data
  • 2
    Each mission gets a unique JOB-XXXXXXXX ID for full traceability
  • 3
    Processing time varies by AOI size, date range, and cloud coverage
  • 4
    Status updates automatically every few seconds — no refresh needed
Step 5 of 5
View Results & Download Reports
↓
Once status shows COMPLETED, your data is ready:
  • M
    Toggle Processed Layer in the sidebar to overlay results on the map
  • T
    Use the Time Scrubber at the bottom of the map to step through date snapshots
  • P
    Click DOWNLOAD PDF to get your evidence-grade report with SHA-256 provenance hash
  • H
    All past missions are listed in Mission History — click any entry to reload it
